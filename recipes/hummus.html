WANA style Hummus

Description

Hummus (traditionally coming from WANA region) is a dip, a spread, or savory dish made from cooked, mashed chickpeas blended with tahini, lemon juice, and garlic. The standard garnish in the includes olive oil, a few whole chickpeas, parsley, and paprika.

In WANA cuisine, it is usually eaten as a dip, with pita bread. In the West, it is now produced industrially, and is often served as a snack or appetizer with crackers.

Ingredients

  • 240g canned chickpeas
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1 tablespoon tahini
  • 1 table spoon of oil
  • 1 table spoon water
  • 1/2 lemon
  • cumin
  • salt
  • chili

Topping

  • 50g brown lentils
  • 50g chickpeas
  • 20g carrot
  • 5 tablespoons oil
  • 4 dates
  • Ras el hanout mix
  • curry mix

Steps

  1. Rinse the canned chickpeas and boil in water until soft. Remove the water and wait until they are cooled down.
  2. Add the cooled down chickpeas together with garlic, tahini, lemon juice, water and spices. Blend until you have a smooth consistency. If needed, add more water, lemon juice, tahini & spices.
  3. Add to a plate and let it rest.
  4. Chop carrots and dates into fine pieces.
  5. Fry the carrots in the oil, add dates after carrots look a bit fried. Add lentils, chickpeas and spices and keep frying. Make sure there is enough oil, if needed add some oil.
  6. When done, top the hummus with the mix, make sure that everything is covered with oil. Let it rest a bit. Eat with pita, fresh bread or what is to your preference.
  7. Enjoy!
